Documentation

A water fund is a source water protection mechanism that pools funding from downstream beneficiaries to invest in upstream source water protection activities. Water funds have four common characteristics: science-based plans, a multi-stakeholder approach, a funding mechanism and implementation capacity. The Water Funds Explorer allows users to learn about operational water fund programs of The Nature Conservancy and its partners.

Filter funds by downstream population

The downstream population benefitting from upstream source water protection activities for a given water fund, as reported by that water fund’s program manager(s); usually corresponds to the size of one or more cities.

Filter funds by number of partners

The number of partner organizations or groups collaborating on a given water fund, as reported by that water fund’s program manager(s). Number of partners does not equate to size or success of a water fund.

Filter funds by activities

The main categories of source water protection activities undertaken by a given water fund, as reported by that water fund’s program manager(s). Categories are defined in data documentation.

Filter funds by benefits

The main categories of benefits anticipated as a result of a given water fund’s activities, as reported by that water fund’s program manager(s). Categories are defined in data documentation.
